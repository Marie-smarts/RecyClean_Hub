New to recycling?

Everything you need to know to get started

Recycling well is a skill — and it matters. Contaminated or unsorted waste can't be processed, which means it ends up in landfill anyway. These quick answers help you do it right from the start.

Start Recycling

Different materials require completely different recycling processes. When plastic, paper, and metal are mixed together, the entire batch is often rejected by recycling facilities and sent to landfill instead. Sorting at source — at home, before you drop off — is what makes recycling actually work.

Currently RecyClean Hub accepts plastic (PET bottles, containers), paper and cardboard (newspapers, office paper, flattened boxes), metal (rinsed cans, tins), glass bottles, and aluminium cans. Each drop-off host lists exactly which materials they accept so you always know before you go.

Three simple rules: rinse food and liquid residue from containers, flatten plastic bottles and cardboard boxes to save space, and separate materials into different bags before you leave home. A clean, sorted drop-off takes less than five minutes to prepare.

Your materials are weighed and logged at the drop-off host. When enough has accumulated (20kg triggers a pickup), a Certified Aggregator collects the batch and transports it to a verified recycling company. There, plastic becomes new packaging, paper becomes cardboard, metal is melted and reformed. Your waste genuinely becomes something new.

When your materials are sold to a recycling company, the platform automatically calculates your share based on weight and material type. 70% of the sale value goes to you, 10% to the drop-off host, and 15% to the Certified Aggregator. Payment records are generated digitally for every transaction — transparent and verifiable.
